Fluid Pressure

Hydrostatic pressure is the pressure applied when the fluid at rest is a result of gravity. It is caused by the mass of the fluid pushing onto a particular point in the fluid and grows roughly with depth beneath the surface. As depth gets deeper, the underlying fluid produces an increased downward force, causing an upward increase in tension at the bottom.

Interactive Illustration

When you move the slider, the water volume increases or decreases. As the volume changes, the pressure at the top, middle, and bottom of the container also varies accordingly. A larger volume results in greater depth and higher pressure at the lower points, while a smaller volume reduces the pressure throughout the fluid.

Water level: 50%

Formula

P = ρ × g × h

Where:

ρ = density
g = acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)
h = height
