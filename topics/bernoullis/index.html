Bernoulli's Principle

Understanding Fluid Dynamics and Pressure

Bernoulli's Principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid causes a decrease in its pressure or potential energy. This principle, introduced by Daniel Bernoulli in 1738, explains the relationship between fluid motion and pressure, and is fundamental in understanding flight, fluid flow through pipes, and many engineering applications.

Key Concepts

  • Pressure Energy: Energy stored in a fluid due to its pressure
  • Kinetic Energy: Energy of fluid motion (½ρv²)
  • Potential Energy: Energy due to height (ρgh)
  • Conservation: Total energy remains constant along a streamline

Bernoulli's Equation

P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant
P Pressure (Pa)
ρ Fluid Density (kg/m³)
v Velocity (m/s)
g Gravity (9.8 m/s²)
h Height (m)
