Understanding Bernoulli’s Equation

Bernoulli's Principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid causes a decrease in its pressure or potential energy. This principle, introduced by Daniel Bernoulli in 1738, explains the relationship between fluid motion and pressure. One common example is a venturi tube, where, as air moves through the narrowest part of the tube, it speeds up and the pressure drops. In aviation, Bernoulli's Principle is important for understanding how lift acts on an aircraft wing. Air above a curved top surface of the wing moves faster than the air beneath the wing, thereby generating lower pressure above and greater pressure below.

Bernoulli’s Equation Formula

P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant

P = Pressure (Pa)

ρ = Density (kg/m³)

v = Velocity (m/s)

g = 9.8 m/s²

h = Height (m)
