Archimedes' Principle

Understanding Buoyancy and Floating Forces

Physical law of buoyancy, discovered by the ancient Greek mathematician and inventor Archimedes, stating that any body completely or partially submerged in a fluid (gas or liquid) at rest is acted upon by an upward, or buoyant, force, the magnitude of which is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the body.

Key Concepts

  • Buoyant Force: The upward force exerted by a fluid on an object
  • Displacement: Volume of fluid pushed aside by the submerged object
  • Floating: Occurs when buoyant force equals object's weight
  • Sinking: Occurs when object's weight exceeds buoyant force

The Formula

Fb = ρ × g × V
Fb Buoyant Force (N)
ρ Fluid Density (kg/m³)
g Gravity (9.8 m/s²)
V Displaced Volume (m³)
