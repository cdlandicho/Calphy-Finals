Archimedes' Principle

Understanding Buoyancy and Floating Forces

Archimedes' Principle states that any object fully or partially submerged in a fluid is pushed upward by a buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced. This principle explains why objects float or sink depending on their density.

Key Concepts

  • Buoyant Force: Upward force exerted by a fluid.
  • Displacement: Amount of fluid pushed aside by the object.
  • Floating: Occurs when buoyant force equals an object's weight.
  • Sinking: Happens when weight is greater than buoyant force.

The Formula

Fb = ρ × g × V
Fb Buoyant Force (N)
ρ Fluid Density (kg/m³)
g Gravity (m/s²)
V Displaced Volume (m³)
