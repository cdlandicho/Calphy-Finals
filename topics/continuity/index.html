Continuity Principle

The Continuity Principle states that for an incompressible fluid flowing in a pipe, the amount of fluid entering one end will be equal to the amount flowing out of the other end in any given amount of time, which preserves mass. The fluid's density is constant, and since a pipe is always full, the mass flow rate is constant in every segment of the pipe.

Formula

A₁V₁ = A₂V₂

A₁ = cross-sectional area of the first section
V₁ = velocity of the fluid at the first section
A₂ = cross-sectional area of the second section
V₂ = velocity of the fluid at the second section
