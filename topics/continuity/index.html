Continuity Principle

Understanding Mass Conservation in Fluid Flow

The Continuity Principle states that for an incompressible fluid flowing through a pipe, the mass flow rate remains constant. When a pipe narrows, the fluid’s velocity increases to maintain the same flow — illustrating how fluids adapt to changing cross-sectional areas.

Fluid accelerates through a narrower section of the pipe — the same volume passes every second.

Key Concepts

  • Mass Conservation: Mass in = Mass out
  • Velocity Change: Narrower pipes increase speed
  • Area-Velocity Relationship: A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
  • Incompressible Flow: Constant density

Continuity Equation

A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
