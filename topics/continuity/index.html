Continuity Principle

Understanding Mass Conservation in Fluid Flow

The Continuity Principle states that for an incompressible fluid flowing in a pipe, the amount of fluid entering one end will be equal to the amount flowing out of the other end in any given amount of time, preserving mass. Since the fluid's density is constant and a pipe is always full, the mass flow rate is constant in every segment.

Key Concepts

  • Mass Conservation: Mass entering equals mass leaving
  • Velocity Change: Narrower pipes increase fluid velocity
  • Area-Velocity Relationship: A₁V₁ = A₂V₂
  • Incompressible Flow: Constant density throughout
